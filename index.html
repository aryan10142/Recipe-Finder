From the Kitchen
Chef's secret tips
Techniques that transform everyday cooking
🔪

Master Your Knife Skills

A sharp knife is 10× safer than a dull one. Curl your fingers under (the "bear claw") and let the blade do the work. Uniform cuts = even cooking.

🧂

Salt at Every Stage

Seasoning only at the end is a rookie mistake. Salt as you build — pasta water should taste like the sea, and vegetables release more flavour when salted early.

🌡️

Rest Your Meat

After cooking, rest meat for at least 5–10 minutes. The fibres relax and reabsorb juices — cutting too early sends them all over your board, not into your mouth.

🍳

Hot Pan, Cold Oil

Always heat the pan before adding oil. This prevents sticking, creates better sears, and gives you that gorgeous Maillard crust on proteins and vegetables.

🌿

Fresh Herbs at the End

Dried herbs go in at the start, fresh herbs at the finish. Heat destroys delicate volatile oils in fresh herbs — basil, cilantro, and parsley shine brightest raw.

🍋

Acid Brightens Everything

A squeeze of lemon or a splash of vinegar at the end of cooking brightens, balances, and elevates any dish. If something tastes flat — add acid before adding more salt.
